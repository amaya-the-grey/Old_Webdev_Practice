About Me

Hello world! I'm Sydney Andrews, a computational scientist and full-time nerd. I received my M.A. in Physics and Astronomy from Stony Brook University where I did computational research on the nucleosynthesis of core-collapse supernovae. I am interested in all aspects of computation, but I am particularly interested in applying computational methods to high-energy astrophysics. Please Download my CV to learn more.

I also have experience working on back-end development, some minor experience in front end development, and I am currently working as a quantum software engineer at a quantum computing startup.


Research Interests

I am primarily interested in computational physics and methods, high performance computing, core-collapse supernovae, pulsational pair-instability supernovae, gamma-ray busts (short and long), compact object formation, hydrodynamics, magneto-hydrodynamics, radiation transport, nucleosynthesis, the first massive stars, and gravitational waves.

My most recent work is in the nucleosynthesis of core-collapse supernovae, where we used the NuGrid nuclear reaction network to post-process a suite of spherically symmetric explosion trajectories to predict chemical observations in the next galactic supernova.
